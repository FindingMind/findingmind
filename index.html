What is Finding Mind?

A lot of the time, our mind tricks our bodies without even knowing it. We behave and react in a way that doesn’t always have a clear answer, causing us to always ask “why?”; a question that typically has no concrete answer. In the ‘Finding Mind’ short film anthology, we delve into the coexistence of body and mind.

Seniors Olivia Gendron (BFA Acting) and Paul Ippolito (Video Communications) took on the project of a 4-part short film anthology for their Senior Thesis Project. Each film is unique in its writing, direction, genre, and overall execution. This collaborative process called for 5 weeks of shooting, 25 different actors, 11 separate locations, and 6 weeks of extensive editing to create a drama, a comedy, and a horror. The overall result; an introduction to the world of film, experience in all the challenges it presents, and what it means to take on the title of “filmmaker”.

The Films

Body (12 minutes) is a drama. It tells the story of a day in the life of a young woman living with anorexia. Director & Screenwriter Olivia Gendron plays the unnamed young woman as she goes about a seemingly normal day. Through a series of reflective shots, the audience sees the interior struggle that is not transparent in her exterior life.

Marcel Boding, Where Are You? (23 minutes) is a comedy. Joe, Ralph, and Marcel were three seemingly normal, awkward college students. To Joe & Ralph's disbelief, Marcel starts dating a beautiful girl named Mary. However, when they break up, Marcel finds himself hypnotized by Scooby-Dooby Doo. The film tells the story of the boys trying to break the spell and get their roommate back!

Obsession (8 minutes) is a horror. A young woman named Macey spends most of her nights staying up too late watching strange YouTube videos. When she watches a particularly strange video of a hypnotic clown dancing, she begins to see him in her daily life. Is the clown a hallucination of sleep deprivation, or some kind of mystical stalker?

My Friend Dario (5 minutes) is a conversation. In this experimental film, anthology makers Paul Ippolito & Olivia Gendron made a short skeleton of a script, and then improvised the dialogue. In it, a kleptomaniac and his sister try to figure out what to do with a recently stolen lizard.
